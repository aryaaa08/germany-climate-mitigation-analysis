# 🌍 Germany’s Role in Global Climate Mitigation  
### Emissions, Responsibility, and Carbon Inequality (1990–2024)

---

## 📌 Project Overview
This project analyzes Germany’s role in global climate mitigation using long-term emissions data (1990–2024). It combines **data analysis, visualization, and policy interpretation** to evaluate:

- Emissions reduction trends  
- Carbon inequality across countries  
- Climate responsibility and justice  

The project integrates **quantitative analysis with real-world climate policy insights**, making it more than just a dashboard — it’s a data-driven narrative.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze Germany’s CO₂ emissions trajectory over time  
- Compare Germany with major economies (USA, China, India, France)  
- Evaluate per capita vs total emissions (inequality lens)
- Consumption and emissions table
- Green house gas emissions
- Understand the effectiveness of the Energiewende transition

## 📈 Key Insights

### 1. Strong Reduction, But Still High Emitter
- Germany reduced emissions by **~46% since 1990**
- However, per capita emissions remain **~10.25 tonnes**, much higher than global average

### 2. Structural vs Temporary Decline
- Major drops linked to:
  - Post-reunification industrial collapse  
  - Economic slowdowns (2009, 2020, 2023)  
- Not all reductions are due to sustainable transition

### 3. Carbon Inequality
- Germany emits **~8x more per person than India**
- Yet developing nations face higher climate impacts

### 4. Sectoral Imbalance
- Electricity sector improved significantly  
- Transport and buildings show **limited progress**
